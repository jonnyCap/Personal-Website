Hello!

I'm Jonathan, a future Software-developer!
I want to show you my rather short Coding journey so far and tell you a bit about my self...

High school

I originally attended a high school specializing in natural sciences based in Austria, Graz. Fortunately, we had a few opportunities to adjust our school timetable, which allowed me to shift my focus towards Informatics. We could choose specific courses, although some were not voluntary. However, that's where I got my first insights into Web development, ECDL, Game-Programming, Network-technologies as well as Software- and Hardware technology.

Transition to Armed Forces

After high school I applied for a special IT-Eduction at the Armed Forces where only very few people got accepted. Thankfully, I got given a chance but since I had only little experience I felt like I had to prepare myself. In the course of my preparation I started learning how to code properly. At that point I only programmed in in a JavaScript-API and therefore I wanted to try out something different. So I ended up with Java, learned the basic as well as advanced concepts and from there on I just programmed as much as possible to get used to the language and its features.

Austrian Armed Forces

A year after my graduation I had to join the Austrian Armed Forces to complete my basic military service. As mentioned before, I was selected to be one of the few austrian "Cyber-Soldiers". At first we underwent a special training and schooling which took 2 Months. During this time we firstly learned a lot about the military, its morals, rules and so on. And secondly we got insights into more IT-related subjects like Cryptography or the safe-military-network. Afterwards we were spread all over Austria again and worked practically for 4 Months. In that time I worked a lot on Websites and Databases, which helped me a lot on improving my JavaScript- and SQL-Skills.

Transition to studying

Now I had a really good grasp of what to learn and where to start if I wanted to make further progress. I started a couple projects, like my first personal App or this Website. Not to mention the many ones which I did not finish or make public. I also was very interested in learning a lower level programing language like C++ or C, but since I was also doing an internship at the time I could only scratch on the surface of these languages.

Software-engineering and Management - Bachelor Degree

My studies start at October, 2022. The last years I have invested a lot of time to learn by myself and hopefully some guided lessons and a curriculum will help my to answer the questions i could not answer myself. I also made an application for an internship at my university but I haven't received an answer yet, so wish me luck! :D

Team Creator

I created an App with Android Studio and Java,
which allows you to create fair Teams!

Project Idea

I originally came up with the Idea of programming Games. However, after I started a few attempts in Unity with C#, I thought that at the time I would probably profit more from learning Android Studios first. Since I did not have any experience in that field of work I just wanted to do a simple App and started planning.

Planning

Coming up with an creative and not completely overdone Idea was actually way harder than expected. Especially rather simple Ideas were to find everywhere and I could not come up with anything new. However, after a few days it clicked when a friend group of mine was playing volleyball we had some bad issues making fair teams. No one really wanted to choose who they want to play with and I thought an App would be optimal to solve that problem for us. So all I wanted was a simple App that creates a specific amount of fairer Teams. So I started planning and writing down everything on paper first...

Learning Android Studio

The planning stage was done really quickly, since the app was not complex at all and I had a pretty clear Idea of its design. I decided to use Android Studio in Combination with Java and turn my Idea into reality. I used YouTube as my main source and for a couple of days I just started creating and developing new app-projects to test out the features and learn the core functions of the IDE. Although there was still a lot to learn, I decided to get started with the App and continue my learning-journey by just doing.

Finishing the App

Despite the only few functions my app had, it took me quite a while to finish it. Especially at the end a lot of fine tuning had to be done. However, in the end of the day it was absolutely worth it, since it was such a satisfaction when I was finally done. Here are some preview pictures of it:

I setup my Google Player Store Account, payed the fee and uploaded my App. I thought I should be done here but I had to rework the App additional 5 times because some requirements were not met. How I started

Well the first time I thought about a Web-page was at my time at the Austrian Armed Forces. In that particular time I was mostly working with JavaScript and overall involved in Web-development. I was tasked to develop the new intern Website for our department but due to some organizational troubles I never came to publish it or neither show it to my supervisors. Therefore, I decided to save my written Code, reworked it a little and tried to use it for my personal Web-page instead. It looked somewhat like this:

Although initially I was quite happy with it, after a while it just looked boring and I decided to cancel the project and started from scratch.

Inspiration

My initial inspiration for the Web design came from my Logo, which I just casually created using an online Logo-Designer. Since I did not want to pay for it tho, I just designed one myself in Gimp, inspired by the one I made previously and used the colors as Guideline for my Web-page.

Outcome

I did not have a clear plan and just started working, which probably weren't the smartest move but it worked out quite well as I believe! I tried to make a cool interactive Web-page with plain JavaScript, which was challenging at times. In hindsight I probably should have used this project to learn new Libraries like React or AngularJS but it still helped me massively improving my Understanding of JavaScript.

JS Mini-Games

A little and honestly really crappy JavaScript-Game-Collection!
I designed and programmed these Games during my time at the Austrian Armed Forces and they honestly helped my a lot to understand the basics of JS

Snake

Snake was the first Game I programmed with JavaScript. At the time I was completely unfamiliar with the language and thats the reason why I did not use anything like Canvas or other helping frameworks or libraries. I simply made a grid filled with colored Divs to display either the background or the snakes Body. Every tail-Div is saved in an array and every few milliseconds a function is called which which paints all divs black except the divs reserved for the snakes body which are painted green.

Flappy Birds

This Game is where I wanted to start experimenting with a randomly created Surrounding, Object-collision and gravity. I did not completely finish this Game and when I stopped working on this game the gravitation and the graphics felt rather unsatisfying to be honest.

Builder

Builder allows you to place forms, in different shapes and sizes wherever on the screen. Then you could pick them up and replace them somewhere else. This game helped particularly in getting a sense of the relative locations of each divs and how to combine that with the mouse-X and mouse-Y position. This was rather a test tho and could not seriously be called a real game.

World Runner

This game is the first game created with HTML-canvas but there is not much I want to share here because there is probably going to be a completely reworked version of this Game. This is probably the Game I put the most thought and effort into. The simple Idea: You run around a 2D world, which is extremely minimalistic. Thats all I am going to say so far:).
If you are interested in the Game you can sign up for News in the Footer!
